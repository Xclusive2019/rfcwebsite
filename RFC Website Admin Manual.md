# RFC Website — Admin Panel User Manual

---

## Welcome

This manual covers everything you need to manage your website from the admin panel. No technical knowledge is required — if you can use email, you can use this panel.

---

## 1. Logging In

1. Open your website and go to the admin panel by adding `/admin` to the end of your website address (e.g. `www.rfcsa.co.za/#/admin`).
2. Enter your email address and password, then click **Sign In**.
3. You will land on the Dashboard.

> If you forget your password, contact your website developer to have it reset.

---

## 2. The Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home screen. It gives you a quick snapshot of:

- **Total bookings** received
- **Pending bookings** waiting for your response
- **Published blog posts**
- **Contact messages** received
- A list of the most recent bookings at a glance

Use the **left-hand sidebar** to navigate between the different sections.

---

## 3. Bookings

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Bookings**

This page shows every consultation request submitted through your website.

### Understanding the status colours

| Colour | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Yellow — Pending | New request, not yet actioned |
| Green — Confirmed | You have confirmed this booking |
| Red — Cancelled | The booking has been cancelled |

### Filtering bookings

At the top of the page, click the buttons to filter by: **All**, **Pending**, **Confirmed**, or **Cancelled**.

### What you can do with each booking

Click **View** next to any booking to open the full details (name, email, phone, company, service, date, time, and any notes they left).

From the list or the detail panel you can:

- **Confirm** — marks the booking as confirmed
- **Cancel** — marks the booking as cancelled
- **Delete** — permanently removes the booking from the system (use this only when you no longer need the record)

> Changes take effect immediately. The customer is not automatically emailed when you confirm or cancel — you would need to contact them separately.

## 4. Managing Your Availability (Blocking Days)

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Availability**

This is where you control which days and times customers can book. Any slot you block will be greyed out and unavailable when someone tries to make a booking on your website.

Weekends and South African public holidays are always blocked automatically — you do not need to do anything for those.

### Step 1 — Choose a date

On the left, you will see a list of upcoming working days. Dates that are already fully blocked show a **"Closed"** badge in red. Click any date to select it.

### Step 2 — Use Quick Block (the easy way)

Once you select a date, a **Quick Block** panel appears at the top with three options:

| Button | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Block Day** | Closes all time slots for just that one day |
| **Unblock Day** | Re-opens all time slots for that day |
| **Block Week** | Closes all working days in the same week |
| **Unblock Week** | Re-opens all working days in that week |
| **Block Month** | Closes every working day in that month |
| **Unblock Month** | Re-opens every working day in that month |

> Example: Going on leave for two weeks in July? Click any day in the first week, click **Block Week**, then click a day in the second week and click **Block Week** again.

### Step 3 — Fine-tune individual time slots (optional)

Below the Quick Block panel, you will see all the time slots for your selected day (08:00 through 16:00). Each slot shows either:

- **Green tick** — available (customers can book this time)
- **Red block icon** — blocked (customers cannot book this time)

Click any individual slot to toggle it between available and blocked. This is useful if, for example, you want to keep most of Monday open but block out the 13:00 slot for a lunch meeting.

## 5. Blog Posts

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Blog Posts**

This is where you write and manage all the articles that appear on your website's blog.

### Viewing your posts

You will see a table of all posts. Each row shows the title, category, whether it is featured, and whether it is published or still a draft.

### Writing a new post

1. Click the green **New Post** button (top right).
2. Fill in the details:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Title** | The heading of your article |
| **Slug** | This fills in automatically — it becomes the web address for the article. You can leave it as-is |
| **Cover Image** | Click the dashed box to upload a photo from your computer (JPG, PNG or WebP, under 5 MB), or drag and drop an image onto it |
| **YouTube / Video URL** | Optional. Paste a YouTube link to show a video player at the top of the article |
| **Excerpt** | A short one or two sentence summary that appears on the blog listing page |
| **Category** | Choose the most fitting category from the dropdown |
| **Content Pillar** | Choose the broader topic area this article falls under |
| **Read Time** | Type how long the article takes to read, e.g. `5 min` |
| **Author** | Defaults to "RFC Team" — change if needed |

3. Tick **Featured post** if you want this article to appear highlighted on the blog page.
4. Write your article content in the large **Content** box at the bottom.
5. To save as a draft (not yet visible to the public), leave **Published** unticked.
6. To make it live on the website, tick **Published**.
7. Click **Create Post** to save.

### Editing an existing post

Click **Edit** next to any post in the list, make your changes, then click **Save Changes**.

### Publishing or unpublishing a post

On the posts list, the status button in the **Published** column shows either "Published" (green) or "Draft" (grey). Click it to toggle — no need to open the editor.

### Marking a post as Featured

Similarly, click the button in the **Featured** column to toggle a post on or off the featured list.

### Deleting posts

- To delete one post: click **Delete** next to it and confirm.
- To delete several at once: tick the checkboxes next to each post, then click the red **Delete X selected** button.

> Deleted posts cannot be recovered. If you are unsure, unpublish the post instead of deleting it.

## 6. Writing Content (Quick Guide)

The blog editor uses a simple format called Markdown for the content area. Here are the basics:

| What you want | How to type it |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Heading** | `# Big Heading` or `## Smaller Heading` |
| **Bold text** | `**bold**` |
| **Italic text** | `*italic*` |
| **Bullet list** | `- Item one` (new line for each item) |
| **Numbered list** | `1. First item` (new line for each) |
| **A link** | `[link text](https://website.com)` |
| **A YouTube video** | Paste any YouTube link on its own line, e.g. `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ABC123` — it will appear as a video player |
| **A line divider** | `---` |
| Normal paragraph | Just type normally, leave a blank line between paragraphs |

## 7. Products (Shop)

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Products**

This page manages the products shown in your online shop.

### Adding a product

1. Click **Add Product**.
2. Fill in the product code, name, packaging, price, and any other details.
3. Upload an image for the product.
4. Click **Save**.

### Editing a product

Click **Edit** next to any product, update the details, and save.

### Activating or deactivating a product

Each product has an **Active** toggle. When turned off, the product is hidden from the public shop but not deleted — useful if an item is temporarily out of stock.

### Deleting a product

Click **Delete** next to the product. This permanently removes it.

---

## 8. Contacts

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Contacts**

This page shows all messages sent through the contact form on your website. You can read each submission here. This is a read-only view — replies must be sent from your own email.

---

## 9. Quote Requests

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Quotes**

Every time a visitor requests a quote from your shop, it appears here. You can see what products they are interested in, quantities, and their contact details. Reply to them directly via email.

---

## 10. Quiz Results

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Quiz**

This shows the results from the audit readiness quiz on your website. For each completed quiz you can see the person's name, email, score, and how they answered each question. Read-only — no changes can be made here.

---

## 11. AI Chat Sessions

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Chat**

A log of conversations visitors have had with your website's AI chat assistant. Useful for understanding what questions your visitors are asking most often. Read-only.

---

## 12. Training Sessions

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Training**

Manage the training sessions shown on your website's training page. Add upcoming sessions with dates, times, locations, and registration links so visitors can sign up.

---

## 13. Settings

**Go to:** Sidebar → **Settings**

### Email Notifications

This is the email address where all website notifications are sent — new bookings, contact messages, quote requests, and quiz completions.

- **Notification Email** — change this to whichever email address you want to receive alerts at.
- Below the email field, you will see four toggles. Switch any of them off if you no longer want to receive notifications for that type of submission.

### External System Links

These are the web addresses that power the action buttons on your website. If any of these external services change their web address, you can update it here without needing developer help.

| Setting | What it controls |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Comply Cloud System URL** | The "Access the System" button on the Comply Cloud page |
| **Virtual Training Register URL** | The default "Register" link for virtual training sessions |
| **E-Learning URL** | Where the E-Learning option sends visitors |

After making any changes on this page, click **Save Settings**.

---

## 14. Logging Out

Click your name or the logout option at the bottom of the sidebar to sign out. Always log out when using a shared or public computer.

## Quick Reference

| I want to… | Go to… |
|------------|--------|
| See new bookings | Bookings |
| Confirm or cancel a booking | Bookings → View → Confirm / Cancel |
| Block a day off | Availability → select date → Block Day |
| Block a whole week | Availability → select a day in that week → Block Week |
| Write a new blog post | Blog Posts → New Post |
| Take a post offline temporarily | Blog Posts → click "Published" badge to set to Draft |
| Add a new product | Products → Add Product |
| Hide a product temporarily | Products → toggle Active off |
| Change my notification email | Settings → Notification Email |
| Update a button link on the website | Settings → External System Links |
| Read contact form messages | Contacts |
| Read quote requests | Quotes |
