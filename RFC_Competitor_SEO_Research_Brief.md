## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

RFC operates in a **moderately competitive but fragmented market** for food safety consulting and training in South Africa. The competitive landscape is dominated by **ASC Food Safety Consultants** (ascfoodsafety.com), which has established a commanding SEO presence through comprehensive content marketing, a two-site strategy (consulting + training), and extensive article/guides that rank for virtually every high-value keyword in the sector.

**Key Findings:**
- **The content gap is the biggest opportunity.** No competitor has a truly dominant blog presence, but ASC and Entecom are investing heavily in long-form guides and articles. RFC has NO visible blog on either site.
- **The R638 training market is the most contested space** with clear price competition (R879 at ASC vs R1,600 at RFC vs R3,393 at Entecom).
- **FSSC 22000 V7 transition (released May 2026) is a major content opportunity** - most sites have thin content on this.
- **Lead generation tactics across the industry are surprisingly weak** - few competitors use pop-ups, exit-intent offers, or sophisticated funnels. This is RFC's opportunity.
- **Local SEO for Pretoria/Gauteng is under-optimized by most competitors** - ASC focuses on Gqeberha/Eastern Cape.

**Top 5 Priorities for RFC:**
1. Launch a blog/content hub on rfcsa.co.za targeting R638, HACCP, and COA keywords
2. Create a lead magnet (free R638 compliance checklist or COA application guide)
3. Build dedicated landing pages for each service + city combination
4. Expand rfcacademy.co.za course catalog (currently only 3 courses vs ASC's 65+)
5. Implement technical SEO fixes (mobile optimization, site speed, schema markup)
